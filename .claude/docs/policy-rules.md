# SunSmart Policy Rules — Extracted Reference

> Source: Three NIWA/Cancer Society NZ template policy documents (July 2025 v2)
> Extracted: 2026-03-20
> Purpose: Drive the policy action engine in the webapp

---

## Universal Trigger

**UVI ≥ 3** → All five sun protection measures must be applied.

All three policy types are identical on this threshold. The five measures are:
1. **Shade** — seek and use available shade
2. **Clothing** — sun-protective clothing
3. **Hat** — compliant hat (see below)
4. **Sunscreen** — SPF 30+ broad-spectrum water-resistant
5. **Sunglasses** — meeting AS/NZ Standard 1067

**Year-round protection** (regardless of UVI) is required:
- At high altitudes or near reflective surfaces (water, snow)
- For individuals with UV-sensitising conditions or medications
- For extended outdoor periods

## Hat Rules

### Compliant hat types (all three policies)
| Type | Minimum spec |
|------|-------------|
| Legionnaire hat | — |
| Broad-brimmed hat | Minimum 7.5 cm brim |
| Deep crown bucket hat | Minimum 6 cm brim |

### Prohibited
- Baseball caps
- Visors

### No hat consequence
| Policy | Consequence |
|--------|------------|
| Early Childhood | Child must play in shade or indoors |
| Primary/Intermediate | Student must play in shade or indoors |
| Secondary | No explicit consequence stated |

### Other
- ECCs provide spare sunhats
- Cultural/religious hat exemptions: must emphasise shade, sunscreen, sunglasses as alternatives

## Sunscreen Rules

### Specification (all three policies)
- SPF 30 minimum
- Water-resistant
- Broad-spectrum
- Meets AS/NZ Standard 2604
- Cream, lotion, or gel preferred — aerosols not recommended (uneven application)

### Application schedule
| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Initial application | At least **20 minutes before** going outdoors |
| Reapplication | Every **2 hours**, or more frequently after sweating or swimming |
| Routine breaks | Provided during outdoor activities for reapplication |
| Sunscreen must not | Be used to justify extending outdoor time |

### Who applies / consent
| Policy | Who applies | Consent required? |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Early Childhood | Children encouraged (under staff supervision); staff may assist | **Yes — written parental permission** required for staff application |
| Primary/Intermediate | Students encouraged to apply independently | Not required |
| Secondary | Students self-directed | Not applicable |

### Supply
- EC: Centre provides sunscreen (family provides if child has allergies)
- P/I: School or family provides
- Secondary: School or family provides

### Special rules — Babies (Early Childhood only)
- Under 12 months: **must be kept out of direct sun** when UVI ≥ 3
- Protection priority: shade → clothing → hat → sunscreen (secondary for babies)
- Under 6 months: widespread sunscreen not recommended
- 6–12 months: sunscreen on small exposed areas only; use sensitive/children's formulation; patch test first

## Outdoor Activity Rules

- Outdoor activities are **encouraged year-round** (vitamin D, health benefits)
- **Never cancelled** due to UV alone — only **rescheduled** if no shade is available
- If shade is unavailable: reschedule to a lower-UV time
- Policy applies **on-site and during excursions** (P/I and Secondary explicitly)
- EC: policy must form part of risk assessments for off-site activities

---

## Clothing Rules

### All policies
- Loose-fitting, covers as much skin as possible
- Tops with sleeves and collars encouraged
- Knee-length or longer shorts/skirts encouraged
- Rash tops for outdoor swimming and water play

### Policy differences
| Policy | Framing | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Early Childhood | Expectation ("children wear") | Some cover-up clothing provided by centre |
| Primary/Intermediate | Expectation ("children wear" if no uniform) | Families encouraged to dress appropriately |
| Secondary | Encouraged ("students encouraged to wear") | Sports uniforms must incorporate sun protection when updated |

---

## Shade Rules (all policies identical)

- Shelters, trees, and shade areas must provide **sufficient coverage for all** students/children and staff
- Students/children encouraged to **use shade** when outside
- Shade must be **factored into planning** for outdoor activities, gatherings, excursions
- If shade is insufficient: reschedule outdoor activities
- Leadership must **plan to increase shade** where lacking
- New constructions and remodels: shade must be considered **from the start of planning**

---

## Sunglasses

- Recommended where practical
- Must meet **AS/NZ Standard 1067**

---

## Staff Obligations (all policies)

- Staff must **follow all the same rules** as students/children
- Staff must **model SunSmart behaviour**

## UVI Severity Scale Reference

Standard UV index categories used in NZ:

| UVI | Category | Colour |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 0–2 | Low | Green |
| 3–5 | Moderate | Yellow |
| 6–7 | High | Orange |
| 8–10 | Very High | Red |
| 11+ | Extreme | Violet |

**SunSmart policy activates at UVI 3** (start of Moderate).

## Webapp Action Engine — Rules Matrix

When UVI ≥ 3, the following actions apply. Differences by policy type noted.

### Always show (all policy types, UVI ≥ 3)
1. Seek and use shade
2. Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen **20 minutes before** going outside
3. Reapply sunscreen **every 2 hours** (or after swimming/sweating)
4. Wear sun-protective clothing (sleeves, collar, knee-length)
5. Wear sunglasses (AS/NZ 1067)
6. Wear compliant hat (legionnaire, broad-brim ≥7.5cm, or bucket ≥6cm)

### Early Childhood extras
- Babies under 12 months: **keep out of direct sun entirely**
- Staff must have **written parental permission** before applying sunscreen
- No hat → child plays in shade or indoors
- Provide spare sunhats for children without one

### Primary/Intermediate extras
- No hat → student plays in shade or indoors
- Rash tops for water play/swimming

### Secondary extras
- Rash tops for swimming sports / outdoor water activities
- No explicit hat consequence (advisory only)
